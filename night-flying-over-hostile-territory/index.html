The Wartime Travels and Adventures of Flight Sgt. George Heritage

It wasn’t all lounging around in the tents and bars of Cairo and Malta. These locations were used as bases for strikes at the ‘soft underbelly’ of Europe. George manned the rear gun of his Wellington bomber - shooting at enemy aircraft as they attempted to chase them from the skies.

The following photos are of bombs dropped on Tobruk and the occupied Island of Crete.
