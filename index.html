The Wartime Travels and Adventures of Flight Sgt. George Heritage

Through the middle east, North Africa, and Europe.

Home About Contact Additional Material

George Heritage was born in Liverpool in 1920, and joined the Royal Air Force as soon as war was declared in 1939. over the next six years, he served in, among other places, North Africa, Malta, and Italy, as well as undergoing training in Scotland & Norfolk.

This site aims to be an easily accessible record of his life, and and specifically his travels during the war years - from the top of the Great pyramid at Giza to 20,000 ft above Italy.

Education
George Heritage is a scholar in the 4th class (Std 7 A section). He was 14 years of age on Sept. 4th 1934. His conduct is very good and he is an intelligent worker. He is willing, obedient and has been a capable house captain, monitor, and has always been found honest & trustworthy.

War in the Middle East and North Africa
A collection of wartime photos taken while on duty and at leisure in the hot places of the world. George was based on Malta and in Egypt, acting as a rear gunner in a Wellington bomber as it attacked Axis installations in North Africa, Italy, and the occupied Greek Islands.

Night Flying over Hostile Territory
Aerial photos of bombs dropping over the Mediterranean

Flight logs
Log books are used to keep records of events and training. While we only have one of George's logbooks, they give clues to day-to-day life of a RAF rear gunner in the early 1940s.
